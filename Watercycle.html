WATER CYCLE

1. Evaporation

Evaporation occurs when the sun heats up water in rivers, lakes, or oceans, turning it into vapor or steam. This water vapor rises into the atmosphere.

2. Condensation

Condensation happens when water vapor in the air cools down and turns back into liquid, forming clouds.

3. Precipitation

Precipitation occurs when the water droplets in clouds become too heavy and fall back to Earth as rain, hail, sleet, or snow.

4. Interception

Interception is the process where precipitation is intercepted by vegetation and never reaches the ground.

5. Infiltration

Infiltration is the process by which water on the ground surface enters the soil, replenishing groundwater reserves.

6. Percolation

Percolation refers to the movement of water through the soil and its layers, eventually reaching the water table.

7. Transpiration

Transpiration occurs when water is absorbed by plant roots and then released as water vapor through pores in leaves.

8. Collection

Collection is the process where water gathers in rivers, lakes, oceans, and other bodies of water. This water will eventually evaporate again, continuing the water cycle.
